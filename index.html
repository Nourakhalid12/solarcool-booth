The Solution

How The System Works

Three components working in perfect synergy — turning the sun's own movement into a free cooling engine.

🔬
① Capillary Tubes on the Metal Frame
Tubes with 0.5–1.5mm diameter are mounted directly on the non-generating metal frame. The metal's high thermal conductivity transfers heat from the cell to the liquid with exceptional efficiency. Heat exchange area exceeds traditional methods by 3–5×.
5× Heat Exchange Area
🫧
② Flexible Balloon as a Hydraulic Pump
Instead of a separate electric pump, the system harnesses the solar panel's own tracking movement. When the panel rotates, one balloon compresses while another expands — pushing coolant through all capillary tubes. 100% free cooling.
Zero Electricity Consumed
🎯
③ Dual-Function Solar Tracking
The tracking motor does two jobs simultaneously: optimizes solar radiation angle (+25–30% extra energy) and pumps the coolant via balloons (zero additional electrical cost). One motor. Two functions.
2 Functions, 1 Motor

Innovation Points

What Makes Us Unique

INNOVATION / 01
Completely Free Pumping
Using tracking movement to circulate coolant is an idea not documented in scientific literature in this form — a genuine original innovation.
INNOVATION / 02
Flexible Balloon as Self-Pump
Eliminates complexity and removes all failure points compared to traditional mechanical liquid pumps.
INNOVATION / 03
Cooling the Metal Frame Only
Preserves the purity of the glass cell and avoids turbidity and sedimentation issues documented in scientific research.
INNOVATION / 04
Dual-Function Motor
Tracking + pumping in one motion — saves the cost of an entire component and raises overall system efficiency.
INNOVATION / 05
Parallel Capillary Distribution
Ensures uniform cooling across the entire panel surface — solving the non-uniform cooling problem documented in peer-reviewed literature.
IMPACT / Community
